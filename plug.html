The Plug

the sites worth knowing — price 'em, grade 'em, find 'em.

Straight from the source

P4K on eBay ebay.com/usr/playinforkeeps34

The official Playin' for Keeps store — doubles, trade bait, and cards that earned their exit. Buy from the wall itself.

 P4K on COMC comc.com/Users/playinforkeeps34

The COMC storefront — singles priced to move, ready to ship or stack with the rest of your cart.

What's it worth?

130point 130point.com

Free lookup of actual eBay sold prices — including the best-offer amounts eBay hides. First stop for "what's this really going for?"

 eBay Sold Listings ebay.com

Search any card, filter to "Sold Items," and you're looking at the real market — not what sellers wish they'd get.

 Card Ladder cardladder.com

Price history charts and market tracking for the bigger cards. The stock-ticker view of the hobby.

Getting cards graded

PSA psacard.com

The biggest name in grading — the red-label slabs. Their free pop report shows how many of a card exist in each grade.

 Beckett Grading (BGS) beckett.com

Known for subgrades — corners, edges, surface, centering scored separately. The black-label 10 is the hobby's white whale.

 SGC gosgc.com

Fast turnaround and the tuxedo-black slabs that make vintage cards look right at home.

Checklists & card catalogs

Trading Card Database tcdb.com

Community-built catalog of nearly every card ever printed. The reference for figuring out exactly which parallel you're holding.

 Beckett Database beckett.com

The old-school authority — set checklists and their price guide, still the standard for cataloging the hobby.

 Cardboard Connection cardboardconnection.com

Set profiles, release calendars, and breakdowns of what's inside every new product before you buy a box.

Where the cards are

COMC comc.com

Check Out My Cards — millions of singles, ship them in one box whenever you're ready. Where a lot of this collection came from.

 eBay ebay.com

Still the ocean everything swims in. Saved searches are the move — let the cards come to you.

 Sportlots sportlots.com

The bargain bin of the internet — base cards and commons for pennies. Perfect for chasing down set fillers.

The community

Blowout Forums blowoutforums.com

The hobby's town square — news, arguments, scam warnings, and some of the sharpest collectors around.

 r/basketballcards reddit.com

Reddit's basketball card corner — mail days, pickups, and honest "what's this worth?" answers.

 Net54 net54baseball.com

The vintage crowd — pre-1980 baseball knowledge that runs deeper than anywhere else. Ernie Sr. territory.
